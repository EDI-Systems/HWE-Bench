"Please design a circuit that must include the following two modules:

The first is a precision signal conditioning and amplification module. This module is designed to interface with a thermal sensing element that generates microvolt-level potential differences proportional to temperature changes. It must include a multi-stage passive filtering network to suppress high-frequency electromagnetic interference coupled from the power switching environment. The core function is to linearly amplify these minute, slow-moving thermal signals into a voltage range compatible with a logic controller’s input. Throughout the system’s operation—from the initial ambient state through the rapid heating phase to the final thermal equilibrium—this module must ensure high signal-to-noise ratio and maintain a stable, non-saturating output that accurately reflects the real-time thermal state of the load.

The second is a high-side power modulation and load driving module. This module acts as the primary power gateway between a high-voltage DC supply and a high-current resistive heating load. It must implement a dual-stage driving architecture where a low-power logic command modulates a high-side power switch. The design must prioritize fast transition states to minimize power dissipation during the 'start-to-steady' PWM cycles. Additionally, it must incorporate an integrated energy clamp or discharge path to safely manage inductive flyback energy during the rapid deactivation of the power path, ensuring that the entire system remains within safe operating limits during every switching transient from power-on to system shutdown."
