Please design a portable, battery-powered digital FM radio circuit capable of receiving audio broadcasts and driving a loudspeaker. The working principle relies on a central microcontroller orchestrating the system over a digital communication bus. It interfaces with a dedicated digital FM tuner module to lock onto radio frequencies and an OLED screen to provide visual feedback to the user. Power management is seamlessly handled by a dedicated charging circuit that safely replenishes a lithium-ion battery from a USB Type-C input, while a linear voltage regulator ensures a stable power supply rail for all sensitive digital logic components.

The complete operational flow begins when the power switch is activated, allowing the battery or USB source to energize the system. When a user interacts with the tactile control buttons to adjust the volume or change the station, the microcontroller processes these physical inputs, sends the corresponding digital commands to the tuning IC, and updates the display. The FM tuner then demodulates the selected radio frequency and outputs a low-level analog audio signal. Finally, this audio signal is conditioned through a passive filtering network and fed into an audio power amplifier, which significantly boosts the signal's amplitude to successfully drive the connected speaker.
