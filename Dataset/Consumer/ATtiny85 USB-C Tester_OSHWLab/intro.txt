Please design a USB Type-C Power Delivery Tester based on an ATtiny85 microcontroller, designed to monitor and display real-time voltage and current from USB-C power sources. The circuit functions as a diagnostic tool that measures power flowing through a precision $8\text{m}\Omega$ shunt resistor toward a connected load. Its working principle relies on a current and power monitor for high-side sensing, while a linear voltage regulator maintains a steady system voltage rail by regulating the input voltage from the power source.
The complete operational process begins with power entering via the USB-C plug or connectors and passing through the sensing stage before reaching the output terminal. The sensing IC digitizes analog measurements and communicates with the microcontroller via an I2C bus. The microcontroller then processes these signals to calculate real-time metrics, displaying the results on an OLED screen. Additionally, users can interact with the device using tactile switches for system reboots or toggling display modes through the controller’s interface.
