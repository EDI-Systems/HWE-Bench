Please design a miniature wireless avionics and flight control board suitable for micro aerial vehicles or high-power attitude-tracking devices. The core architecture must integrate a wireless central processing unit, a comprehensive spatial awareness sensor suite (including multi-axis inertial measurement and atmospheric pressure detection), a robust power management ecosystem, high-current drive channels for inductive loads, and an automated wired diagnostic bridge for seamless firmware flashing. 

The operational workflow begins with the power management ecosystem, which safely charges a single-cell battery and employs a step-up followed by a step-down regulation topology. This ensures a stable logic voltage is maintained even when sudden motor activations cause severe battery voltage drops. Once initialized, the central processor continuously aggregates real-time orientation and altitude data from the sensor suite. Based on these dynamic inputs, the processor executes flight or stabilization algorithms and translates them into precisely timed pulse signals for the high-current drive channels, governing the rotational speed of external motors. Concurrently, the wired diagnostic bridge handles external communications and utilizes an automated hardware-toggling logic to force the main processor into a programming state, facilitating effortless firmware updates without any physical user intervention.
