Please design a smart power management and control system for an electronic cuckoo clock. The core workflow of the entire system revolves around the intelligent routing of power and the safe isolation of high-power loads. During the "Power Path Mux" stage, the circuit utilizes an automatic switching mechanism to seamlessly select the optimal power source between the external USB and the built-in lithium battery; when external power is connected, the system automatically cuts off the battery discharge loop and takes over the global power supply, and when the external power is disconnected, it instantly and seamlessly switches back to battery power. This process entirely prevents voltage conflicts and reverse current, ensuring the continuous and stable operation of the main controller, audio, real-time clock, and display modules.

When executing physical actions, the system enters the "Load Switch" management process. To prevent the massive inrush current at the moment the servo motor starts from dragging down the main power supply and causing the main controller to reset, the power supply channel for the motor is independently isolated. The microcontroller normally keeps it powered off to save energy, and only when the motor needs to be driven will it send a logic signal to turn on the dedicated electronic load switch. This delivers electrical energy—buffered by a large capacitor—specifically to the motor, and immediately cuts it off after the action is completed, thereby achieving highly efficient and stable coordination of the entire device.
