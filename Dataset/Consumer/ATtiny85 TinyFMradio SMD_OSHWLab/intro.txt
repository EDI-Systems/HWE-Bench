please design a compact and portable FM radio system centered around an ATtiny85 microcontroller. The architecture must integrate several critical modules: an RDA5807MP FM tuner IC for radio signal reception, an XPT8871ES audio amplifier to drive an external speaker, an I2C-based OLED screen for visual feedback, a mechanical rotary encoder for user interface, and a dedicated 3.3V linear regulator (HT7333-A) to ensure stable power distribution across the digital logic and RF components.

The system workflow initiates with the user interacting with the rotary encoder to tune stations or adjust volume. The ATtiny85 captures these physical inputs through hardware-debounced RC filters and translates them into I2C control commands directed simultaneously to the OLED display (for UI updates) and the RDA5807MP tuner. Upon receiving the frequency command, the FM tuner demodulates the RF signal and outputs a baseband analog audio stream. This dual-channel audio signal is then AC-coupled and routed into the XPT8871ES power amplifier, which boosts the signal strength to directly drive the speaker, completing the seamless signal path from digital user input to analog acoustic output.
