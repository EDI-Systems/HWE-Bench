Please design a portable, wireless-enabled digital audio player equipped with a visual interface and battery management. The system architecture is built upon several critical modules: a central wireless microcontroller for data processing and network communication, a high-fidelity digital-to-analog audio converter, a removable storage interface, a graphical screen, a multiplexed analog keypad, and a power regulation subsystem. The fundamental working principle involves the central processor fetching compressed digital audio streams—either streamed over a wireless network or read from local storage—and transmitting them via a synchronous digital audio bus to the dedicated audio converter. This converter then translates the digital data into a clean, low-noise analog signal capable of driving standard headphones directly.

The operational workflow is driven by user interactions through the analog keypad, which cleverly utilizes a resistor network to interpret multiple distinct button presses via a single analog sensing line. Once a playback command is issued, the main processor updates the visual display with relevant track metadata and orchestrates the continuous, jitter-free flow of audio data to the decoding stage. Concurrently, the power management module operates autonomously in the background; it safely handles the charging cycle of the internal battery when connected to an external source, while a dedicated linear regulator steps down the voltage to provide tightly regulated, noise-filtered power to both the sensitive analog audio circuits and the high-speed digital components.
