Please design a highly reliable flight control and navigation motherboard for advanced unmanned aerial vehicles. The core architecture must feature a wireless central processing unit dedicated to real-time flight dynamics, paired with a highly redundant sensory array. This array should incorporate multiple independent inertial measurement units, dual barometric altimeters, and a discrete magnetometer to ensure fault-tolerant spatial awareness and orientation tracking. Additionally, the system must provide robust peripheral interfaces, including multi-channel output headers for motor speed controllers, and dedicated communication buses for remote control receivers, satellite positioning modules, and a high-bandwidth data bridge to dock seamlessly with an onboard companion computer for advanced autonomous pathplanning.

The operational workflow begins with the redundant sensing array feeding high-frequency spatial telemetry to the central processor. The processor fuses this kinematic data with positional inputs from the satellite module and manual commands from the remote receiver. Concurrently, it maintains a continuous, high-speed data exchange with the docked companion computer, offloading complex visual or navigation algorithms while receiving immediate trajectory corrections. Finally, the central processor translates these fused navigational directives into precise, microsecond-level digital control signals sent to the multi-rotor motor drives, ensuring stable and highly responsive airborne maneuvering.
