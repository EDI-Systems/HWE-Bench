Please design a portable wireless audio receiver capable of capturing digital audio streams and driving external headphones or analog speakers. The system should integrate several critical modules: a central wireless processing unit, a power management and battery charging sub-system, an analog audio coupling network, and a user interface. The core working principle relies on a highly integrated main processor that captures wireless radio frequency signals or reads local storage media, decodes the compressed digital audio data, and performs digital-to-analog conversion. Simultaneously, the power management module ensures safe operation by routing external wired power to recharge an internal battery while supplying a stable voltage to the system, allowing for seamless transition between tethered and portable use.

The complete operational workflow begins when the user toggles the main power switch, energizing the system from either the internal battery or an external power source. Upon initialization, the central processor establishes a wireless pairing with a host device and begins receiving the digital audio payload. The processor decodes this stream and converts it into raw left and right analog audio channels. To protect the connected listening devices, these analog signals are routed through a passive capacitive filtering network to block any harmful direct current offsets before reaching the final audio output jack. Throughout playback, the user can adjust the volume or skip tracks using a set of tactile buttons, which generate distinct voltage levels that the processor interprets to execute the corresponding control commands.
