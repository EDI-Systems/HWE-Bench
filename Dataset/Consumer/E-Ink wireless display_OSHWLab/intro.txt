Please design a portable, battery-operated wireless controller board dedicated to driving a large electronic paper display. The system architecture must comprise a comprehensive power management ecosystem, a central wireless processing unit, a dedicated display interface, and a wired diagnostic bridge equipped with an automated firmware-flashing mechanism. The power ecosystem should feature a dedicated charging circuit to safely manage a rechargeable battery via a standard serial interface, alongside a dual-rail voltage regulation network: one branch stepping the battery voltage down to feed the low-voltage digital logic, and the other stepping it up to generate the high-voltage baseline required to drive the electronic ink screen.

The operational workflow centers around the wireless processing unit, which maintains a low-power state until it fetches new image data over a wireless network. Once data is received, the processor formats the graphics and orchestrates the high-voltage display interface to physically refresh the screen. Simultaneously, for development and maintenance, the wired diagnostic bridge translates external communication signals and utilizes an automated hardware-toggling sequence to seamlessly force the main processor into a programming state, allowing users to update the system firmware without manual intervention.
