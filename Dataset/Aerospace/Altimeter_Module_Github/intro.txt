Please design a circuit that integrates a high-output acoustic signaling system and a dual-channel pyrotechnic recovery controller for aerospace applications. The signaling subsystem must employ an H-bridge topology driven by complementary pulse-width modulation (PWM) signals to effectively double the peak-to-peak voltage across a piezo transducer， while incorporating a dual-input power architecture that allows for functional audio testing even when the primary flight battery is disconnected. 

For mission-critical recovery deployment, the module must utilize high-current P-channel MOSFET switches to regulate the ignition of primary and drogue electronic matches directly from the main battery bus. To ensure launch readiness, a continuity-sensing network should be integrated using a voltage divider principle to monitor the electrical integrity of the ignition loops， allowing the microcontroller to verify the presence of functional pyrotechnic charges via its analog-to-digital converter before flight.
