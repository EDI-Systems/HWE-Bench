Please design a circuit that integrates a high-current pyrotechnic deployment channel with an acoustic status indicator for an aerospace recovery system. The deployment channel operates as a low-side switching stage where a power MOSFET regulates the connection between a high-voltage battery bus and an external initiator; it features an integrated continuity-sensing circuit composed of a voltage-divider network that translates the terminal’s electrical state into a low-voltage feedback signal for real-time monitoring by a microcontroller. 

Simultaneously, the acoustic indicator uses a signal-level MOSFET to drive a piezo buzzer via pulse-width modulation, providing audible telemetry and recovery beacons. Both subsystems are designed with safety pull-down resistors at their control interfaces to ensure the switching elements remain in a non-conductive state during system power-up or microcontroller initialization, effectively preventing premature ignition or unintended noise.
